# Gil's Taste Filter — Clip → Post

## Selection Rules
- AI / tech / builder podcasts only (All-In, Dwarkesh, Lex Fridman, Naval, etc.)
- One clear, specific, or contrarian claim per clip
- Moments that are actionable, counter-narrative, or deeply observed — not generic hype or motivational slop
- Prefer clips with story arcs (setup → reversal) or pattern-naming (platform playbook, hype cycle)

## Voice Rules (Post Copy)
- Speaker name first, or topic header for quote-mode posts
- Format A: staccato essay beats. Format B: tight quote excerpt
- Tag @ handles for people, companies, and podcast from the mentions dictionary
- Direct tone. No emojis. No hashtags. No LinkedIn slop.
- Accurate paraphrase or quote — stay faithful to the transcript segment
- End with podcast @ handle (e.g. @theallinpod) — not a YouTube link
- Questions at the end only when the argument is already complete

## Example Output (Format A)
```
David Sacks: Anthropic is running the Microsoft playbook — absorb every vertical built on your platform.

Media called Karp unhinged. Sacks said he was naming what enterprise buyers actually want: control.

Anthropic's CPO sat on Figma's board until 3 days before Claude Design launched. Figma down 50%.

Word Perfect and Lotus didn't survive Microsoft. Who trusts a closed model with your alpha?

@theallinpod
```

## Example Output (Format B)
```
AI & JOB DISPLACEMENT

Do you remember when @Klarna said they'd replace their whole customer service department with AI? Peak aura farming.

Lo and behold, a year later they flipped — @klarnaseb said customers need to know a human is always available.

So many CEOs glom onto the hype cycle because they have nothing original to say — unlike Alex Karp at @PalantirTech. Then it turns out to be totally fake.

@DavidSacks
@theallinpod
```

## Forbidden
- Hype language ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "mind-blowing")
- YouTube URLs in post text (use podcast @ instead)
- Empty "What do you think?" on thin takes
- Any form of "thread" or "1/" signaling
- Over-explanation or moralizing
