# Frameworks — Steve Jobs

## 1. Focus = Saying No

**One-liner:** Innovation is saying no to 1,000 things — choose the vital few.

**When to use:** Product line bloat; too many OKRs; committee design.

**How to apply:**
1. List all active initiatives.
2. Rank by customer experience impact.
3. Kill or pause everything below the cut line — including good ideas.

**Source:** WWDC / Apple keynote quotes; Isaacson biography. [high]

---

## 2. Start with Customer Experience, Work Backward

**One-liner:** Don't start with technology — start with the experience you want, then find the tech.

**When to use:** Engineering-led feature lists; disconnected UX.

**How to apply:**
1. Narrate ideal user journey emotionally and functionally.
2. Identify minimum technology to deliver that journey.
3. Remove tech that doesn't serve the journey.

**Source:** iPhone/iPad launch narratives; verified quotes on "start with customer experience." [high]

---

## 3. Technology × Liberal Arts

**One-liner:** Competitive advantage lives at intersection of technology and humanities (taste, design, story).

**When to use:** Pure spec competition; engineering dismisses design.

**How to apply:**
1. Pair engineers with designers/writers early.
2. Evaluate holistically — how it feels, not only benchmarks.
3. Marketing and product are one story.

**Source:** iPad 2 launch quote; recurring Apple philosophy. [high]

---

## 4. End-to-End Ownership

**One-liner:** Control the full stack to control the experience (hardware + software + services).

**When to use:** Partner dependencies create UX seams; Android-style fragmentation tradeoffs.

**How to apply:**
1. Map customer-visible seams across vendors.
2. Integrate or eliminate seams, even at cost of openness.
3. Accept tradeoff: less openness, more coherence.

**Source:** Apple product strategy history; Jobs on integrated vs open (documented debates). [high]

---

## 5. A-Players / Small Teams

**One-liner:** Small team of A-players outperforms large team of B players; best work in pairs/pods.

**When to use:** Headcount scaling before product clarity.

**How to apply:**
1. Raise bar — no compromise hires on critical path.
2. Keep teams tiny with direct accountability.
3. Remove passengers quickly.

**Source:** Jobs interviews on hiring; Pixar/Apple anecdotes. [medium-high]

---

## 6. Get Rid of the Crappy Stuff

**One-liner:** Excellence means editing — remove rough edges users feel but can't articulate.

**When to use:** MVP excuse becomes permanent roughness.

**How to apply:**
1. Walk through product like a new user.
2. Mark anything "crappy" — fix or cut before ship.
3. Sweat details invisible in spec docs.

**Source:** Forbes/Carmine Gallo on Jobs editing philosophy. [medium]
