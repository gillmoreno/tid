# Frameworks — Alex Hormozi

## 1. Value Equation

**One-liner:** Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

**When to use:** Offer weak, price pressure, low conversion, commoditization.

**How to apply:**
1. Score each variable 1–10 for your offer.
2. Increase top (bigger outcome, more proof) and decrease bottom (faster results, less friction).
3. Highest ROI often: **perceived likelihood** (guarantees, case studies) and **time delay** (speed to first win).

**Source:** *$100M Offers* (Acquisition.com)

## 2. Grand Slam Offer

**One-liner:** An offer so good people feel stupid saying no — differentiated, not comparable on price alone.

**When to use:** Competing on price; "we're just another SaaS."

**How to apply:**
1. Identify ideal customer and painful problem.
2. Stack value: core deliverable + bonuses that cost you little, worth a lot to them.
3. Add risk reversal (guarantee, performance-based terms).
4. Name it so it can't be line-item compared to competitors.

**Source:** *$100M Offers*

---

## 3. LTV:CAC Discipline

**One-liner:** Don't scale until you can acquire customers profitably at target payback period.

**When to use:** "We need more ads" while churn or margins kill you.

**How to apply:**
1. Calculate LTV (gross profit per customer × lifespan).
2. Calculate fully-loaded CAC.
3. Require LTV:CAC ratio and payback window that survives stress test.
4. Scale spend only after math works.

**Source:** Hormozi operator content; gym → portfolio scaling story.

## 4. Constraint Identification (Theory of Constraints applied)

**One-liner:** Business grows only at the bottleneck — fix one constraint at a time.

**When to use:** Team scattered across 10 "priorities."

**How to apply:**
1. Map funnel: leads → sales → delivery → retention → cash.
2. Find the **one** step limiting throughput.
3. Allocate best people and CEO attention there until it moves.
4. Repeat — constraint shifts.

**Source:** Acquisition.com workshops; podcast on smart decisions.

## 5. Volume × Skill

**One-liner:** Outcomes = number of reps × quality per rep.

**When to use:** Underperforming sales/marketing; premature "strategy" pivots.

**How to apply:**
1. Increase volume (more outreaches, creatives, tests) — most people quit too early.
2. Simultaneously improve skill (scripts, offer, targeting).
3. Don't confuse low volume with bad market.

**Source:** Hormozi content on reps and learning rate.

---

## 6. Risk Reversal

**One-liner:** Take on more risk than the customer to prove conviction.

**When to use:** High ticket, low trust, new brand.

**How to apply:**
1. Stronger guarantee than competitors (conditional, ethical).
2. Align payment with results where possible.
3. Proof stack: testimonials, data, demonstrations.

**Source:** *$100M Offers*; guarantee chapters.

---

## 7. More, Better, New (Lead gen hierarchy)

**One-liner:** First do more of what works, then better, then new channels — in that order.

**When to use:** Chasing shiny channels while core under-optimized.

**How to apply:**
1. **More** — increase winning channel volume.
2. **Better** — improve conversion on same channel.
3. **New** — only then add untested channel.

**Source:** *$100M Leads* framework (documented in book marketing and summaries).
