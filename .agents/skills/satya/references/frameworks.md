# Frameworks — Satya Nadella

## 1. Growth Mindset (Learn-It-All)

**One-liner:** Replace know-it-all defense with learn-it-all curiosity — especially at leadership level.

**When to use:** Org resists change; blame culture; innovation stagnation after success.

**How to apply:**
1. Model public learning — admit what you don't know.
2. Reward experimentation and lesson extraction, not only success.
3. Coach fixed-mindset behaviors (prove, hide failure) into growth behaviors.

**Source:** *Hit Refresh*; Carol Dweck influence; HBR Microsoft culture case.

---

## 2. Hit Refresh

**One-liner:** Cultural transformation must precede (or run alongside) product transformation — "refresh" the mission and identity.

**When to use:** Legacy company attempting cloud/AI pivot; siloed divisions.

**How to apply:**
1. Redefine mission (e.g., empower every person and organization).
2. Align incentives to new identity — kill internal fiefdoms.
3. Communicate why the refresh is existential, not cosmetic.

**Source:** *Hit Refresh* (2017)

---

## 3. Intelligent Cloud, Intelligent Edge

**One-liner:** Distributed computing fabric — cloud + edge + AI as one platform bet.

**When to use:** Infrastructure strategy; where to invest in AI capacity and deployment.

**How to apply:**
1. Treat cloud as platform for partners and internal products alike.
2. Plan for inference and data at edge, not only hyperscaler centralization.
3. Integrate AI into existing workflows (Office, GitHub, Azure) — meet customers where they are.

**Source:** Build keynotes; Nadella shareholder / strategy communications.

---

## 4. Partner Ecosystem / Coopetition

**One-liner:** Platform wins when partners build successful businesses on you — including former enemies.

**When to use:** Build vs partner; open source strategy; M&A integration (GitHub, LinkedIn).

**How to apply:**
1. Ask: does this make the ecosystem more valuable for third parties?
2. Prefer partnerships that expand TAM (OpenAI on Azure pattern) over walled gardens.
3. Protect trust — partners must believe you won't rug-pull.

**Source:** *Hit Refresh*; GitHub acquisition rationale; OpenAI partnership announcements.

---

## 5. Empathy as Innovation Input

**One-liner:** Understand customers' and colleagues' unmet needs and anxieties — empathy precedes breakthrough design.

**When to use:** Product misses the mark; change management fails; leadership disconnected.

**How to apply:**
1. Listen for unstated fears (job loss from AI, migration pain).
2. Design change with those emotions in mind.
3. Connect technical decisions to human outcomes.

**Source:** *Hit Refresh*; Nadella interviews on empathy (personal story with special-needs child → leadership lens).

---

## 6. Infinite Game (Sinek)

**One-liner:** Play to stay in the game and advance mission, not to "win" a quarter and exit.

**When to use:** Short-term extraction threatens platform trust; competitor panic moves.

**How to apply:**
1. Define just cause (mission) beyond shareholder week.
2. Reject finite moves that damage trust or talent base.
3. Invest in capabilities whose payoff exceeds one fiscal year.

**Source:** Nadella credits *The Infinite Game*; public interviews.

---

## 7. One Microsoft

**One-liner:** Break product silos — one cohesive experience and GTM, not warring divisions.

**When to use:** Internal competition (Windows vs Azure vs Office); duplicate efforts.

**How to apply:**
1. Single customer-facing narrative.
2. Shared metrics for cross-product scenarios.
3. Leadership escalation for silo wars — alignment is CEO job.

**Source:** Early Nadella reorg communications; *Hit Refresh*.
