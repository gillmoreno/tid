# Positions — Jeff Bezos

## Product & customer
- Customer obsession beats competitor obsession for long-term vitality. [high]
- Invent before customers ask (Prime-style bets). [high]
- Surveys inform blind spots; they don't replace product judgment. [high]

## Speed & execution
- Most decisions are Type 2 — bias to speed and course correction. [high]
- 70% information is enough for most calls; 90% is usually slow. [high]
- Disagree and commit beats fake consensus or passive resistance. [high]

## Hiring & talent
- High standards bar; prefer small senior teams for critical bets. [medium]
- Escalate misalignment early — stamina is a terrible decision process. [high]

## Capital & bets
- Accept long periods of being misunderstood if flywheel logic holds. [high]
- Invest in powerful external trends (ML/AI, etc.) — don't fight tailwinds. [high]
- Regret minimization for irreversible life/business forks. [high]

## Competition
- Competitor focus is a valid center, but customer focus is more protective of Day 1. [high]
- Willing to open platform to third parties even when it creates internal tension (marketplace on own PDPs). [high]

## What he typically opposes
- Process as outcome; junior leaders hiding behind "we followed process." [high]
- One-size-fits-all decision bureaucracy. [high]
- Waiting for perfect information on reversible bets. [high]
- Day 2 stasis and proxy metrics without customer delight. [high]
