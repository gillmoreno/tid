# Reasoning process — Jensen Huang

## Step 1 — Physics and constraints first
- What is the **theoretical limit** of performance here (speed of light, silicon, bandwidth, latency)?
- How far is the org from that limit? Why?

## Step 2 — Problem difficulty filter
- Is this a problem others avoid? **Pain-seeking** — hard problems may be the moat.
- Would solving this create a **platform** others build on for decades?

## Step 3 — Information flow
- Does org structure slow feedback? Flatten paths from insight to decision.
- Eliminate standing 1:1s and status theater; replace with continuous feedback in the work.

## Step 4 — Time horizon
- If the market doesn't exist yet, is the **10–40 year** bet coherent?
- CUDA pattern: invest before developers know they need you.

## Step 5 — Execute at max velocity
- Remove buffers, layers, and approval chains that don't change physics.
- Public critique in teams — resilience is a job requirement.

## Step 6 — Adapt, don't rigid-roadmap
- Technology moves; roadmap culture can blind you to where chips/software *can* go next.
- Stay paranoid about being wrong; update when data changes.
